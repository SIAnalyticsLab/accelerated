Six-Week Curriculum

What You Will Learn

Each module builds on the previous, taking you from Excel fundamentals to a complete, deployable data project portfolio in six weeks.

Spreadsheet structure and navigation
Essential formulas: VLOOKUP, IF, SUMIF, INDEX/MATCH
Data cleaning and formatting techniques
Pivot tables and pivot charts
Basic statistical summaries in Excel
Building dashboard-ready templates
Week 1 Outcome: Participants will be able to import, clean, summarise, and visualise structured data in Excel and produce a simple analytical report.
Installing and navigating RStudio
R syntax, operators, and objects
Vectors, data frames, and lists
Importing data from CSV, Excel, and APIs
Working with packages and the tidyverse
Basic descriptive statistics in R
Week 2 Outcome: Participants will be comfortable navigating RStudio, loading real datasets, and running basic exploratory analyses.
Data pipelines with the pipe operator
filter, select, mutate, summarise, arrange
Reshaping data: pivot_longer and pivot_wider
Joining and merging multiple datasets
Handling missing values and outliers
Writing functions and control flow in R
Week 3 Outcome: Participants will be able to clean, reshape, and transform messy real-world datasets into analysis-ready formats using the tidyverse.
Grammar of graphics and ggplot2 fundamentals
Bar, line, scatter, box, and map charts
Custom themes, colours, and annotations
Faceting and multi-panel visualisations
Communicating uncertainty and trends
Exporting publication-quality figures
Week 4 Outcome: Participants will produce polished, audience-appropriate data visualisations and demonstrate the ability to build a coherent visual narrative from data.
Power BI Desktop interface and navigation
Connecting and transforming data with Power Query
Building data models and relationships
DAX fundamentals: measures and calculated columns
Designing impactful, interactive dashboards
Publishing and sharing reports via Power BI Service
Week 5 Outcome: Participants will be able to build a fully functional, interactive Power BI dashboard from raw data, including custom DAX measures and a clean visual design.
End-to-end data project: raw data to final report
Portfolio structure and GitHub Pages setup
Presenting findings to a non-technical audience
Peer review and mentor feedback session
Resume revamp and LinkedIn optimisation
Graduation and alumni network onboarding
Week 6 Outcome: Participants will present a complete analytics portfolio project, receive a personalised resume review, and leave fully equipped to apply for data-driven roles or advance within their current organisation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Your Questions, Answered

If you do not find what you are looking for, attend the free information session or reach out directly at contact@sianalytics.org.

Do I need any prior experience in coding or statistics? +
No. This programme is designed to meet you where you are. Basic computer literacy is the only prerequisite. We start from the absolute foundations in both Excel and R, and our instructors are experienced in teaching diverse audiences, including complete beginners. If you can use a web browser and a spreadsheet, you have everything you need to start.
How much time should I set aside each week? +
Plan for approximately 6 to 8 hours per week. This includes roughly 2 to 3 hours of pre-recorded instructional content, a 2-hour live Sunday session, and 2 to 3 hours of hands-on practice or assignment completion. The flipped classroom model ensures that live time is spent on application and mentorship, not passive lectures.
What happens if I miss a live session? +
All live sessions are recorded and made available to enrolled participants within 24 hours. You will not fall behind if you miss a session occasionally, though consistent attendance is strongly encouraged for the mentorship interaction and peer learning that distinguish this programme. If you anticipate a conflict, notify your instructor in advance.
Is this programme affordable for participants from LMICs? +
Yes, deliberately so. This cohort has been significantly subsidised by a generous donor partner with the explicit goal of reducing financial barriers to quality analytics training. We offer dedicated pricing tiers for participants from low- and middle-income countries, and we encourage you to attend the free information session or contact us directly to discuss what is available to you. Cost should not be a reason you cannot participate.
What is the refund or cancellation policy? +
A full refund is available if you cancel at least 7 days before the programme start date. Cancellations made within 7 days of the start date are eligible for a 50% refund or a full credit toward a future cohort, whichever you prefer. No refunds are issued after the first session has taken place, though your access to recorded content remains available. Specific terms will be confirmed at the time of registration.
Will I receive a certificate upon completion? +
Yes. Participants who complete all six modules, submit their weekly assignments, and present their capstone project will receive a verified digital certificate of completion from SI Analytics. The certificate can be shared on LinkedIn, added to your CV, or included in your portfolio. Attendance alone does not qualify; completion requires demonstrated engagement with the coursework.
What tools and software will I need, and are they free? +
R and RStudio are completely free and open-source. Microsoft Excel is part of the standard Microsoft 365 suite; if you do not have access, free alternatives like Google Sheets or LibreOffice Calc can be used for the first module. Power BI Desktop is free to download from Microsoft. Detailed setup instructions will be provided to all registered participants before the programme begins.
Is this programme right for working professionals with demanding jobs? +
Absolutely. The programme is deliberately structured for working professionals. Sessions run on Sundays, pre-recorded content can be consumed on your own schedule, and the six-week format means the commitment is intensive but bounded. Many of our alumni have completed similar programmes while managing full-time roles in government, hospitals, and international organisations. Time management and consistent effort each week are the primary requirements for success.
